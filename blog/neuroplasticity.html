Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

- Posted 13 July 2022, Brayden Drummond

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Before discussing how neuroplasticity benefits people, we first need to know neuroplasticity is. Put simply, neuroplasticity is the idea that the brain has the ability to be modified, changed, and even adapt throughout our lives and any experiences we encounter. Shaw and McEachern write that there are two main perspectives on neuroplasticity:

  • Neuroplasticity is one fundamental process that describes any change in final neural activity or behavioral response, or;
  • Neuroplasticity is an umbrella term for a vast collection of different brain change and adaptation phenomena.

The theory of neuroplasticity is a developing subject, and what we know for certain is that there are two main types of neuroplasticity:

  • Structural neuroplasticity, in which the strength of the connections between neurons (or synapses) changes.
  • Functional neuroplasticity, which describes the permanent changes in synapses due to learning and development (Demarin, Morović, & Béne, 2014).

Now having a basic understanding of neuroplasticity, you may be wondering 'well how does neuroplasticity benefit people?' There are many benefits to neuroplasticity, and studies have shown that neuroplasticity can, among other things: help with depression; allow your brain to recover from traumatic events (such as a stroke); provides for enhanced memory abilities; and provide for more effective and targeted learning.

By understanding what neuroplasticity is and it's benefits that it has on an indivvidual, why would you not want to engage with it? What are some ways to increase neuroplasticity?

There are many ways to increase neuroplasticity, however the two key methods are:

  • Task repitition; and
  • Task-specific practice.

In other words, learning a new skill or activity through specific, regular practice can result in significant changes in the brain.

What does it mean to have a Growth Mindset?

You are not automatically born with a growth mindset, and this can be developed. To sum up what a growth mindset is, it means that an individual believes taht their talents can be developed through hardwork, good strategies, and input from others. People who have a growth mindset tend to achieve more than those with a fixed mindset (that individuals are born with their talents and skills). I know for a fact that when I was researching into this subject, I was surpirsed at the amount of scientific research and evidence which backs up this statement.

Whether you are looking to start a new career, learn a new instrument, or develop a skill, it is important to have a growth mindset (that your skills will be developed through practice and repitition).
